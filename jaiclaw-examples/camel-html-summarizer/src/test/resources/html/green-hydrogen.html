Green Hydrogen: The Missing Piece in Clean Energy

Green hydrogen — produced by splitting water using renewable electricity — is emerging as a critical solution for decarbonizing industries that batteries alone cannot fix. Steel manufacturing, long-haul shipping, and aviation all require energy-dense fuels that lithium-ion batteries struggle to provide. Electrolyzers, the devices that produce green hydrogen, have seen costs drop by 40% in the last five years as manufacturing scales up. Countries like Australia, Chile, and Morocco are positioning themselves as hydrogen export hubs, leveraging their abundant solar and wind resources. The challenge remains infrastructure: pipelines, storage tanks, and refueling stations are expensive and slow to build. But with over $300 billion in announced investments worldwide, the hydrogen economy is no longer a distant vision — it is under construction.
